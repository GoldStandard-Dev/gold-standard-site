Cookie Policy

Updated: December 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how Gold Standard Digital Marketing (“we”, “us”, or “our”) uses cookies and similar technologies on this website. By using this site, you agree that we may use cookies in accordance with this policy unless you disable them in your browser settings or through any cookie preferences we make available.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function properly, remember your preferences, and provide anonymized data about how visitors use the site.

2. How We Use Cookies

We may use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Ensure the site works securely and as expected,
  • Improve loading speed, navigation, and user experience,
  • Understand how visitors use the site and which content is most useful,
  • Measure the performance of marketing and advertising efforts,
  • Support features such as embedded content, analytics, or third-party tools.

3. Types of Cookies We May Use

  • Essential / Functional Cookies – Required for core website functionality, security, and basic features. The site may not function properly without these cookies.
  • Analytics & Performance Cookies – Help us understand how the site is used (for example, which pages are most viewed) so we can improve content and performance.
  • Marketing Cookies – Used to deliver and measure relevant ads, remarketing, and campaign performance.
  • Preference Cookies – Remember choices such as language or display settings to provide a more tailored experience.

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set their own cookies when you visit our site. These may include:

  • Analytics tools (for example, Google Analytics),
  • Advertising and remarketing platforms (for example, Meta / Facebook, Google Ads),
  • Embedded media providers (such as video or social embeds),
  • Marketing or CRM tools that help us understand user behavior.

These third parties may process information about your use of the site in accordance with their own privacy and cookie policies. We do not control how they use their cookies.

5. How to Control or Disable Cookies

You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings. The exact steps depend on your browser, but typically you can find these options under “Privacy” or “Security” settings. For example, you can usually:

  • View which cookies are stored on your device,
  • Delete existing cookies,
  • Block cookies from specific sites,
  • Block all or certain types of cookies.

Please note that disabling some cookies may impact how the website functions and may limit certain features.

6. Consent & Preferences

Where required by law, we will ask for your consent before using non-essential cookies such as analytics or marketing cookies. If a cookie banner or preferences panel is available on this site, you can use it to review and update your cookie choices at any time.

7. Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, legal requirements, or the services we provide. When we make significant changes, we will update the “Updated” date at the top of this page.
